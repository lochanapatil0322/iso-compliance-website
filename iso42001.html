AI Management System

ISO 42001:2023 and AI Governance

ISO 42001 is focused on management system practices for organizations that develop, provide, or use AI systems. It helps structure accountability, risk management, monitoring, and responsible AI controls.

Why AI Governance Matters

AI systems can affect privacy, fairness, security, decision quality, and public trust. A management system approach helps organizations define who is responsible, what risks must be assessed, what evidence should be kept, and how AI systems should be monitored after deployment.

Common AI Risks

Bias and Fairness

AI output may treat groups unfairly if training data, design choices, or monitoring are weak.

Privacy and Data Use

AI systems may process sensitive information, expose personal data, or use data beyond its intended purpose.

Transparency

Users and auditors may need explanations about how AI is used and what limitations exist.

Security

AI systems can be exposed to model abuse, data poisoning, prompt attacks, insecure integrations, or leakage.

Reliability

AI outputs can be incorrect, inconsistent, or unsuitable for high-impact decisions without review.

Accountability

Clear ownership is needed for design, approval, monitoring, incident response, and improvement.

Audit Checklist Areas

  • AI policy and governance roles are defined.
  • AI systems are inventoried with intended purpose and ownership.
  • AI risk assessment considers security, privacy, fairness, reliability, and misuse.
  • Data governance covers source, quality, legality, retention, and protection.
  • Human oversight is defined for important or sensitive AI decisions.
  • Monitoring detects performance drift, incidents, harmful output, and control failures.
  • Improvement actions are tracked and reviewed by accountable owners.
